## 1. The strategy in four sentences

1. **We don't have hardware to ship. We have software that works.** So we ship the software today (7 daemons, MIT, free) and use it as the proof of life.
2. **The category is "proactive on-device AI companion."** That category is empty as of 2026-06-19 02:30 UTC. Every competitor (Meta, Google, Snap, Apple, Acer) is reactive and/or cloud.
3. **The wedge is the timing of the prompt.** Not the model size, not the camera resolution, not the battery life. The agent pushes events to the user at the moment they're needed.
4. **MIT + sub-50g + <$200 BOM is the cell no one else occupies.** Snap ships 132-136g, $2,195. Meta ships reactive, closed. Google ships reactive, cloud. Dan Glasses is the only MIT, proactive, on-device, sub-50g, India-priced entry.

## 3. The five strategic bets

**Bet 1 — Proactive is the category. (HIGH confidence, HIGH risk.)**
- The category is currently empty. Every AI glasses product is reactive. If we own "proactive AI companion" in the public mind before anyone else ships it, we own the wedge for 18-24 months.
- Risk: no one else builds proactive because the model + power budget doesn't allow it. Then the wedge is a feature, not a category.

**Bet 2 — MIT + sub-50g + <$200 BOM is the cell. (HIGH confidence, MEDIUM risk.)**
- Snap is 132g / $2,195. Meta Ray-Ban is ~50g but closed, cloud, reactive. Google Android XR is ~50g but closed, cloud, reactive. **Nobody is in the sub-50g + <$200 BOM + MIT + on-device + proactive cell.** That is a 2x2 cell, and the entire field is on three of the four corners.
- Risk: someone else hits the cell first. Mitigation: the cell is the hardest to hit (requires MIT + sub-50g + <$200 BOM + on-device + proactive), and we are the only team publicly building for it.

**Bet 3 — The Snap $500M number is the lead. (MEDIUM confidence, MEDIUM risk.)**
- $500M is the receipt. The MIT path is the *only* path. If we land the "Snap spent $500M, we spent $0" framing in the public mind, every closed competitor has to defend their burn.
- Risk: too negative. The "vs Snap" framing is a fight with a much bigger company. Mitigation: lead with the cell, not the fight. The cell is what we are; the fight is what they are not.

**Bet 4 — Qualcomm START is the silicon ally. (MEDIUM confidence, HIGH upside.)**
- The first START customer is Inspecs (eyewear OEM). The program is invite-only. If Dan Lab applies, we get free dev kits + co-marketing + a path to a second OEM (Lenskart, Vibe Glass).
- Risk: invitation-gated, slow, requires actual hardware. Mitigation: applying is 1 page + 30 min. If we don't get in, we lose nothing.

**Bet 5 — The Q3 demo is the inflection. (MEDIUM confidence, HIGH risk.)**
- The public demo of Dan Glasses hardware on a real face is the one event that 10x's the channel.
- Risk: hardware slips. Mitigation: ship the Linux-laptop demo video first (July), the face demo second (Q3).

## 4. The seven anti-strategies (what we are NOT doing)

1. **We are NOT building a Discord server.** Not at this stage.
2. **We are NOT writing an "AGI from India" landing page hero.** It's a brand pillar, not a feature.
3. **We are NOT promising a Q4 2026 launch.** Q1 2027 dev-kit alpha is the honest line.
4. **We are NOT running paid ads.** No budget, and the ICP is on X / HN / GitHub where reach is free.
5. **We are NOT hiring a marketing agency.** Dan1 is the marketing department.
6. **We are NOT pivoting the product to chase a competitor.** If Meta ships proactive in 2027, we ship better proactive in 2027. We don't ship *Meta's* proactive.
7. **We are NOT going to be on every social platform.** X + GitHub + LinkedIn + the landing page. That's it for 2026.

## 8. Voice and tone rules

**We are:**
- Direct. "This is what we ship. This is what's next."
- Technical. We don't apologize for jargon. We assume the reader knows what `LLM`, `GGUF`, `whisper.cpp`, and `Q4_0` mean.
- Honest. We name the uncharacterized power draw. We name the unconfirmed BOM. We name the 0-star repo. We name the $0 spent.
- Asymmetric. The story is "from India to the world" — we say it once and never again.
- Confident without arrogance. We are not "the best AI glasses." We are "the only MIT-licensed proactive AI glasses in the sub-50g, <$200 BOM cell."

**We are not:**
- Hype. Never "revolutionary." Never "game-changing." Never "disrupting."
- Apologetic. Never "we think." Never "we hope." Never "we believe."
- Vague. Never "soon." Never "various applications." Never "intelligent assistant."
- Corporate. Never "synergy." Never "leverage." Never "stakeholder."
- **NEW v61:** Bitter. Never "Snap sucks." Never "Meta is evil." The framing is the cell, not the fight. Snap is 132g + $2,195 + reactive. That's a fact. We are sub-50g + <$200 BOM + proactive + MIT. That's a fact. The contrast sells itself.

**The tone test:** if you removed the Dan Lab name from the sentence and replaced it with "Apple" or "Meta," would it still read like Apple or Meta copy? If yes, rewrite it.

## 9. The competitive posture (one paragraph)

We don't compete with Meta Ray-Ban on style. We don't compete with Snap Specs on display. We don't compete with Apple Vision Pro on immersion. We compete on **four axes** — proactivity, on-device, MIT, sub-50g + <$200 BOM — and we own those four axes because the entire field is on zero of them. **The 2x2 cell is ours.** Snap is 132g / $2,195; we are <50g / <$200 BOM. Snap is reactive; we are proactive. Snap is closed; we are MIT. Snap is cloud-dependent; we are on-device. The cell is the wedge; the cell is the story.

**When a competitor ships something good:** we say so once, in one line, on X. We do not write essays dunking. We do not quote-tweet with "actually." We ship.

**When a competitor ships something bad:** we say nothing. Privacy scandals (Meta NameTag), battery problems (Snap Specs 4h), design problems (Apple Vision Pro weight) speak for themselves.
