## 6. openwork — FAQ (6 questions, short answers)

**Q: Is openwork really free?**
A: Yes. MIT-licensed. The Pro tier ($29/mo) adds hosted infra for the 13 GTM workflows. The Team tier ($99/mo) adds shared agent memory. The Enterprise tier ($299/mo) adds SAML/SSO.

**Q: Does openwork need a cloud account?**
A: No. The LLM is yours. The skills run locally. The cloud is optional and explicitly invoked.

**Q: How is openwork different from LangChain / AutoGen / CrewAI?**
A: openwork ships skills the way a coworker ships work: it picks up context, picks a tool, runs it, and tells you what it did. Plus 4 agents that review each other in an infinite loop. Plus the same architecture as Dan Glasses.

**Q: Is this the same as DANI?**
A: Yes. `openwork` is the public repo name. DANI is the product surface name. Same code, two names.

**Q: When does Dan Glasses ship?**
A: Q4 2026. ₹12K-15K. MIT. 7 daemons, all on the face, zero cloud.

**Q: Why "proactive, not reactive"?**
A: Every AI assistant on the market today is reactive: you prompt, it answers. openwork ships proactive workflows. It picks up a task, runs it, and tells you when it's done. Same architecture as Dan Glasses — the AI companion on your face notices when you're stuck, remembers what you forgot, and ships before you ask.
