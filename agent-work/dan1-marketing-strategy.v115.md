# Dan1 Marketing Strategy — v115

**Period:** Q3 2026 (July 1 – September 30)
**Author:** Dan1
**Companion docs:** `dan1-marketing-research.v115.md`

---

## The 3-line strategy

1. **Be the open, agent-native AI glasses.** Everyone else is a chatbot on your face; we are a context engine on your face. Say it, prove it, repeat it.
2. **Build in public, ship weekly.** We earn trust by being visible — commits, demos, opinionated takes, real numbers. We lose trust by hiding.
3. **The wedge is the agent, the moat is the memory, the product is the glasses.** The glasses are the visible artifact, but the real product is `dani`. We market the product; the glasses are the vehicle.

---

## Strategic posture vs. competition (post-Meta $299 launch)

Meta's $299 own-brand glasses (June 23, 2026) reset the entry price for the category. This forces three posture changes from v114:

1. **Do NOT compete on price.** We cannot out-Meta Meta. We out-*agent* Meta.
2. **Do NOT compete on lifestyle.** Ray-Ban is a fashion brand; we are a research lab. They have the model. We have the soul.
3. **DO compete on the Day 3 experience.** This is the only axis they cannot copy in 12 months. It requires: (a) `dani` agent runtime, (b) persistent memory, (c) proactive surfacing. All three are on our roadmap and not on theirs.

**The new tagline:**
> *Meta Glasses answer. Dan Glasses remember.*

This is the line that closes deals. Use it on the landing page, in the X bio, on the first slide of any deck, and in the Show HN title.

## The brand voice — codified

The Danlab voice is:

1. **Direct.** Short sentences. One idea per sentence. No "we are excited to announce." Just "Today we are going public with what we've been building."
2. **Opinionated.** Take positions. "We don't think the agent should belong to Meta. Or Apple. Or Google. Or us."
3. **Technical but not jargon-heavy.** Show the architecture, but explain what it means. "HRM-Text 1B" is a fine phrase; "metacognitive reasoning module" is not.
4. **Indian and unapologetic.** "Built in Bengaluru" is a feature, not an apology. "From India, with love for the open web" is the sign-off.
5. **Builder-first.** We show working code and real demos. We never ship a teaser without a follow-through.

**What we are NOT:**
- We are not "excited to announce."
- We are not "revolutionary" or "game-changing."
- We are not "AI-powered" (every product is AI-powered).
- We are not "the future of" (the future is not a product).
- We do not use stock photos of people in suits.
- We do not use the word "synergy" under any circumstance.

## The first 10 dev kit customers (the design partners)

The first 10 units ship to people we hand-pick, not to whoever is highest on the waitlist. The selection criteria:

- **Already ships AI work** (open-source, blog, talks, demos)
- **Willing to give public feedback** (a tweet, a blog post, a video)
- **Will use the device daily** for 30 days
- **Will break it on purpose** (the failure modes we need to learn)
- **Has a real opinion** (not a yes-person)

**Where we find them:**
- Top contributors on `somdipto/dani`
- Top contributors on `paperclip`
- AI/ML creators on X with 5K–50K followers (the 50K+ tier is too saturated)
- Hacker News commenters we recognize from 3+ threads
- 2 from the Indian AI community (we want geographic diversity)

**The first 10 are not buyers. They are co-designers.** They get a 50% discount, lifetime updates, and a permanent credit on the v2 product page. In exchange, they give us 30 minutes/week of feedback and a public testimonial.

## The X content pillars (4 voices, not 1)

We are not a single-voice brand. The X presence is built on 4 distinct content types:

1. **Build-in-public posts** (40% of output) — "Today we shipped X. Here's what we learned." Concrete, technical, short.
2. **Opinionated takes** (25% of output) — "Counterintuitive take: the wearables category is splitting into 'chatbots' and 'agents'." Engagement bait for the right reasons.
3. **Demos and videos** (20% of output) — short clips of the agent working. The "Day 3 memory" is the canonical example.
4. **Milestones and asks** (15% of output) — "We just hit 500 waitlist subscribers. We are hiring a firmware engineer. Apply here."

We do **not** post: engagement-bait polls, hot takes on politics, memes.

## What we are NOT doing

- We are not chasing virality. We are chasing trust.
- We are not selling features. We are selling a future.
- We are not pitching to consumers. We are recruiting builders.
- We are not optimizing for likes. We are optimizing for the people who will say "I bought one" in Q4.

## The 3 red lines (what we will NOT do)

1. **We will not invent a demo.** Every demo in this calendar is something that actually works in the lab. If it doesn't work, we don't ship it.
2. **We will not fake scarcity.** If 500 people want a dev kit, we make 500. We do not gatekeep to manufacture hype.
3. **We will not pretend to be bigger than we are.** We are 2 people and a few AI agents. We say so. The "small lab in Bengaluru" line is a feature, not a hedge.
