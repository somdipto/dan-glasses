## The Monday Opinion Thread (template, repeat weekly)

Pick one of:
- A new piece of news (Meta/Snap/Google)
- A category claim ("the wearables category is splitting")
- A counter-position to a popular take

5–7 tweets. One claim, defended with evidence. Ends with: "More in the repo. Link below."

**First instance (Mon, Jul 14):**
> Thread on why "AI glasses" is the wrong category, and what the right one is.
>
> 1/ "Smart glasses" means glasses that show you things. The Apple Vision Pro, Snap Specs, Google Project Aura — they all compete on display, resolution, field of view.
>
> That's the wrong race. The race is the agent.
>
> 2/ The agent is what remembers. The agent is what acts. The agent is what you talk to when the screen is off.
>
> Meta is shipping a chatbot. We are shipping a context engine. These are not the same product.
>
> 3/ The categories should be:
> - Display-first: Apple, Snap, Google
> - Agent-first: us, eventually Meta, eventually everyone
>
> We are betting on agent-first. The display is a peripheral, not the product.
>
> 4/ What this means in practice:
> - We do not need a 4K display. We need a good enough one for notifications and directions.
> - We do not need an AR headset. We need good audio and a single eye display.
> - We do need an agent that runs for 8 hours on a 200mAh battery.
>
> 5/ HRM-Text 1B is what makes the agent-first bet possible. A 1B model that runs on a microcontroller. No GPU. No cloud. 8 hours of context.
>
> That's the unlock. Everything else is iteration.
>
> 6/ More in the repo.
> github.com/somdipto/dani
