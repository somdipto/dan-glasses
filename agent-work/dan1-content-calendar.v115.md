## What I'm NOT scheduling (and why)

- **Daily LinkedIn posts.** Lower ROI than X. 1/week is enough.
- **Twitter Spaces / X Spaces.** Audio is high-effort, low-distribution for our ICP. Skip in Q3.
- **Reddit posts.** Engage as a builder, don't shill. We post to Reddit only if a thread directly invites us.
- **Conference talks not at the top 3 AI venues.** Waste of time in Q3; revisit in Q4.
- **Newsletter.** We don't have one yet; the email list is the list. Wait until 1,000 subscribers.

## Measurement — what we track weekly

Every Sunday, we update a single sheet with:

1. **Email subscribers** (cumulative, by source: direct, X, HN, GitHub)
2. **X followers** (cumulative, on `@NandySomdipto`)
3. **X engagement rate** (likes + retweets + replies per post, average)
4. **GitHub stars** (cumulative, by repo)
5. **YouTube views and subscribers** (cumulative)
6. **Show HN status** (if applicable: front page? Top 10? Top 25?)

The dashboard is private, password-protected, and lives at `danlab.dev/admin`.

If a number goes down week-over-week, we ask: did the artifact fail, or did the channel fail? Both have different fixes.

## The "Day 3 memory" video — the most important artifact of the quarter

**Why it matters:** This is the single video that converts a curious viewer into a waitlist subscriber. The video is 60 seconds. It shows:

1. A user wearing Dan Glasses, asking "What's the capital of Mauritius?" on Day 1.
2. The agent answers "Port Louis."
3. Hard cut. Three days pass.
4. The agent, unprompted: "Port Louis just announced a new port development. Want the link?"
5. The user smiles. End of video.

**Production requirements:**
- Filmed on a phone is fine. No studio.
- Subtitles are mandatory.
- Vertical and horizontal versions.
- The user is a real person, not an actor.
- The agent is the real `dani` agent, not a mock.

## What we don't post (the anti-pattern list)

These are the things that kill trust faster than they build it:

- **Engagement-bait polls** ("Which is better, A or B?")
- **Vague teasers** ("Something big is coming...")
- **Cringe founder content** ("Day 47 of building a startup. Today's lesson: ...")
- **AI-generated images** of our product (we use real photos or no photo)
- **Stock photos** of people in suits
- **"We're humbled to announce"** (we are not humbled; we are working)
- **"Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "the future of"** (these are red flags)
- **Testimonials from people we paid** (the 10 design partners are credible precisely because they are not paid)
