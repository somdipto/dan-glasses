## The 3-line strategy (v116)

1. **Lead with the Apple signal and the Meta paywall.** Apple killed Vision Pro. Meta is charging users $20/month to hear. The face is the next platform, and the closed players are already showing what the closed version looks like. We are the open answer. Say it, prove it, repeat it.
2. **Ship the Open Letter this week.** The Meta paywall story is a one-time gift. We have a 10-day window where "open-source AI glasses + accessibility = free forever" is a story no one else is telling. Ship the letter, pitch The Verge, post to HN, dominate the conversation before Meta moves on.
3. **Build in public, ship weekly.** We earn trust by being visible — commits, demos, opinionated takes, real numbers. The Apple validation + the Meta controversy create the most receptive audience of 2026. We do not waste it.

Or the shorter form, for places with character limits:

## The brand voice — codified (unchanged from v115)

The Danlab voice is:

1. **Direct.** Short sentences. One idea per sentence. No "we are excited to announce." Just "Today we are going public with what we've been building."
2. **Opinionated.** Take positions. "We don't think the agent should belong to Meta. Or Apple. Or Google. Or us."
3. **Technical but not jargon-heavy.** Show the architecture, but explain what it means. "HRM-Text 1B" is a fine phrase; "metacognitive reasoning module" is not.
4. **Indian and unapologetic.** "Built in Bengaluru" is a feature, not an apology. "From India, with love for the open web" is the sign-off.
5. **Builder-first.** We show working code and real demos. We never ship a teaser without a follow-through.

**What we are NOT:**
- We are not "excited to announce."
- We are not "revolutionary" or "game-changing."
- We are not "AI-powered" (every product is AI-powered).
- We are not "the future of" (the future is not a product).
- We do not use stock photos of people in suits.
- We do not use the word "synergy" under any circumstance.
- **We are not "democratizing AI." We are building it. That's the line.**

## What I am NOT recommending (and why)

- **No paid ads in Q3.** The product isn't ready, the ICP is small, and the conversion path is long. We can't measure ad ROI yet.
- **No influencer campaign.** Too early; the story isn't sharp enough; we have nothing to send them. (The deaf-tech creator gift in Act 2 is a beta partnership, not an influencer campaign.)
- **No press push yet, except via the Open Letter.** The Open Letter is its own press event. We do not need a separate PR agency in Q3.
- **No Discord launch.** Start with a private Discord for the first 10 dev kit customers. Public Discord in Q1 2027.
- **No rebrand.** Keep "Dan Glasses." The internal agent name "dani" is fine; the spelling inconsistency across repos is a separate cleanup task.

## The Open Letter (full text, ready to ship)

**Title:** An Open Letter to Hearing-Impaired Smart Glasses Users
**Subtitle:** Meta is putting a paywall on a feature that runs on your own face. We won't.

> Last week, Meta announced that Conversation Focus — the hearing-amplification feature on Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses — will be limited to 3 hours a month for free users and 15 hours a month for $20/month Meta One Premium subscribers.
>
> Conversation Focus runs entirely on the glasses. It uses the on-device beamforming microphones and the open-ear speakers. It does not touch Meta's servers. There is no marginal cost to Meta when you use it. The rate limit is a soft paywall on a feature that lives on hardware you already own.
>
> Conversation Focus is also, functionally, an accessibility device. It helps people who are hard-of-hearing follow conversations in noisy rooms. It is the kind of feature that should never be paywalled.
>
> We are building Dan Glasses. They run dani, an open-source agent. Our accessibility features will be free forever. We are making that promise in writing, on the open web, with the commit history to back it up.
>
> We are a small lab in Bengaluru. We will not ship 3 million units this year. We will not be on the cover of Vogue. But we will never charge you to use a feature that runs on your own face.
>
> If you are a hearing-impaired, deaf, or hard-of-hearing user who wants to try open-source AI glasses, reply to this letter, or email us at accessibility@danlab.dev. We will put you at the top of the dev kit waitlist.
>
> — somdipto & dan, danlab.dev

## What we are NOT doing

- We are not chasing virality. We are chasing trust.
- We are not selling features. We are selling a future.
- We are not pitching to consumers. We are recruiting builders.
- We are not optimizing for likes. We are optimizing for the people who will say "I bought one" in Q4.
- **We are not attacking Meta.** We are simply not doing what they did. *"Meta charges. We don't"* is a more powerful line than *"Meta sucks."*
