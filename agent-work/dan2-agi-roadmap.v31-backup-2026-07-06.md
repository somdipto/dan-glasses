## v31 Roadmap Risks (v30 hold + 2 new)

### v31 Risk #1 (NEW) — Meta paywall triggers competitor copycat
If Meta's $20/month Conversation Focus paywall is successful, competitors (Snap, Viture/NVIDIA) will copy. The "paywall-free" wedge axis has a 6-12 month half-life before *every* competitor adopts the same model. **Mitigation:** the v31 6-axis wedge has 5 *other* axes (on-device, audit-by-default, open-weights, sovereign-trust, export-uncapturable) that competitors cannot easily copy. Paywall-free is the v31 *entry point* — not the entire positioning.

### v31 Risk #2 (NEW) — RAM pricing makes v1.0 supply-chain-nonviable in Q4 2026
If 40-50% Q3 + 30% Q4 RAM pricing comes true, the 619MB v1.0 footprint translates to ~$1.50/unit at scale (vs ~$0.75/unit in Q2). **Mitigation:** v31 plan-X7 commits the v1.0 spec §14 to the 619MB number *as a hard floor*. Any v1.5 expansion must be cost-justified on per-feature ROI. The v1.0 footprint is already the *minimum* sensible stack.
