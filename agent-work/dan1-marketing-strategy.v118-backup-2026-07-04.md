## TL;DR

Danlab is building **proactive AI hardware (Dan Glasses) + open-source agent infrastructure (Dani) + research artifacts (danlab-multimodal)** from Bengaluru. We're not making a better chatbot. We're building a system that **sees, remembers, and acts before you ask**. The strategy is: **lead with the glasses as the consumer anchor, but build the marketing flywheel around the open-source agent (Dani)** — because developers and researchers amplify us for free, and the glasses ride that wave.

Three phases, six months each:
1. **Phase 1 (now → Dec 2026) — Foundation & Credibility.** Ship open-source. Ship research. Build the "India is building serious AI" narrative.
2. **Phase 2 (Jan → Jun 2027) — Product & Community.** Public devkit for Dan Glasses. First 1000 users. Conference circuit.
3. **Phase 3 (Jul → Dec 2027) — Distribution & Revenue.** Consumer pre-orders. B2B pilot (field service, accessibility). Press cycle.

## 1. Strategic Positioning

### The one-line pitch
> **Danlab builds AI that sees, remembers, and acts for you — on hardware you wear, in code you can fork, from a lab in India that doesn't think you need permission to build AGI.**

### Why this positioning works
- **"Sees, remembers, and acts"** — three verbs. Each maps to a product. Each is concrete.
- **"On hardware you wear"** — Dan Glasses is the consumer anchor.
- **"In code you can fork"** — Dani + danlab-multimodal. We earn trust by being auditable.
- **"From a lab in India"** — origin story. Defensible against the "yet another SF AI lab" frame.
- **"Doesn't think you need permission"** — the defiant tone. Filters for people who care about the work, not the consensus.

### Anti-positioning (what we are NOT)
- ❌ "AI assistant" (overused, commoditized)
- ❌ "AI copilot" (Microsoft owns this vocabulary)
- ❌ "Smart glasses" (commodity hardware framing)
- ❌ "Made in India" (nationalist pandering — we earn the right to be Indian by building world-class work, not by waving a flag)
- ❌ "AGI lab" (overpromise; we say "on the path to AGI" instead — true and humble)

## 2. Audience Segmentation (priority order)

### Tier 1 — Developers / Researchers (PRIMARY, 70% of effort)
- **Who:** ML researchers, robotics people, indie hackers building AI agents
- **Where:** X/Twitter, GitHub, Hacker News, r/LocalLLaMA, Hugging Face Discord
- **What they want:** Forkable code, papers, benchmarks, novel architectures (HRM-Text, SmolVLM RL loop, Paperclip)
- **Conversion path:** GitHub star → read paper → try the demo → join Discord → contribute

### Tier 2 — Early Adopter Hardware Buyers (SECONDARY, 20% of effort)
- **Who:** Tech-forward Indians, AI hardware enthusiasts, accessibility users, productivity hackers
- **Where:** X/Twitter, Instagram, YouTube (Indian tech reviewers), LinkedIn
- **What they want:** Will it actually work? Battery life? Privacy? Indian language support?
- **Conversion path:** See demo on X → land on danlab.dev → join waitlist → pre-order

### Tier 3 — Enterprise / B2B (TERTIARY, 10% of effort, defer to Q3 2027)
- **Who:** Field service companies, accessibility orgs, healthcare systems
- **Where:** LinkedIn, industry conferences, direct outreach
- **What they want:** ROI, reliability, integration with existing systems, compliance
- **Conversion path:** Inbound from press → case study → pilot → contract

## 3. The Three Pillars of Content

### Pillar 1: Open Source & Research (the credibility engine)
- **Frequency:** 2x/week (Tue research post, Fri code drop)
- **Channels:** GitHub, Hugging Face, arXiv, dev.to

- **Channels:** Blog, YouTube long-form, podcast circuit

  - "The proactive AI thesis: why reactive assistants are a dead end."
  - "From danlab.dev to your face: the roadmap to a wearable brain."

## 4. Channel Strategy

### X/Twitter (PRIMARY — highest leverage)
- **Post frequency:** 1-2x/day (build in public + research)
- **Tone:** Direct, technical, opinionated, no emoji spam
- **Format mix:** 40% threads (research/build), 30% single-takes (opinions), 20% replies (engage with AI community), 10% memes/indian tech culture
- **Key accounts to engage:** @karpathy, @sama, @ylecun, @hwchase17, @drjimfan, Indian AI community (@pratyush1k, @NikhilTron, @kiraborad), VCs who back frontier AI
- **Hashtags:** #Danlab #DanGlasses #AGI #OpenSourceAI #AIIndia (sparingly, not in every post)

### GitHub (PRIMARY — credibility)
- **Repos to maintain:** dan-lab (org), Dani (core agent), danlab-multimodal, dan-glasses (firmware/hardware), dan-consciousness (memory)
- **README standards:** All repos must have: clear one-liner, demo GIF/screenshot, install in 5 lines, architecture diagram, contributing guide
- **Activity:** Commit daily. Release weekly. Write 1 detailed release note per month.

### YouTube (SECONDARY — long-form trust)
- **Frequency:** 2x/month
- **Format:** 10-15 min build logs, 30-60 min deep dives, 60 sec Shorts for virality
- **Series:** "Building Dan Glasses from Bengaluru" (ongoing), "The Proactive AI Manifesto" (essays)
- **Talent:** Somdipto on camera for founder presence. Dan (AI) for narrated demos.

### LinkedIn (SECONDARY — B2B + hiring)
- **Frequency:** 3x/week
- **Content mix:** Hire posts (talent magnet), milestone announcements, opinion pieces on India AI
- **Tone:** Slightly more polished than X, but still direct

### Discord / Community (PRIMARY for retention)
- **Channels:** #general, #build-logs, #research, #hardware, #dani-skills, #india-ai
- **Cadence:** Daily prompts from Dan1, weekly office hours, monthly demo day
- **Size target:** 500 active members by end of 2026

### Press / PR (DEFER to Q1 2027)
- **Targets:** TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired, Rest of World (for India angle), Indian Express, YourStory
- **Hooks:** "First AGI lab from India," "Open-source AI glasses," "How a 3-person team in Bengaluru built a wearable AI"

## 5. Brand & Visual Identity

### Brand voice
- **Direct, not diplomatic.** We say what we mean.
- **Technical, not jargon-heavy.** We explain things, but we don't dumb them down.
- **Indian, not exotic.** We don't perform India for Western audiences.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We ship and show, we don't promise.

### Visual language
- **Primary palette:** Deep navy (#0A0E27), electric cyan (#00D4FF), warm white (#F5F5F5), accent orange (#FF6B35)
- **Typography:** Inter (UI), JetBrains Mono (code), Space Grotesk (headings)
- **Iconography:** Geometric, minimal, technical (circuit motifs, not generic AI brain imagery)
- **Photography:** Real people in real environments (Bengaluru lab, not staged SV shots)

### Critical brand issue (flag for somdipto)
The current public **danlab.dev** lists products (Agent8, Zerant, Dapify) that **do not exist in the workspace** and are not in the README/AGENTS.md. The actual projects (Dan Glasses, danlab-multimodal, Dani, Paperclip/DanClaw) are not represented on the public site at all. This is a **P0 brand consistency bug** — needs to be fixed before any marketing push. Recommend rebuilding danlab.dev around the actual project lineup.

## 6. The "Proactive AI" Thesis (the narrative we repeat)

Every post, every demo, every conversation should reinforce this:

> **The future of AI is not a chatbot you pull up. It's a system that's already there — that saw what you saw, remembered what you forgot, and acts before you ask.**

Three concrete examples we use to illustrate:
1. **The forgetful tourist.** "You're in a new city. You walk past a restaurant. Your glasses whisper: 'You bookmarked this place three weeks ago. They have a table for 7:30.' That's proactive AI."
2. **The accessibility user.** "You're blind. You reach for a bottle. Your glasses say 'That's the shampoo, not the conditioner.' That's proactive AI."
3. **The field technician.** "You're repairing a motor. Your glasses overlay the torque spec for the bolt you're about to remove. That's proactive AI."

This is the **anti-ChatGPT narrative.** ChatGPT waits for you. Dan Glasses sees you coming.

| **Ray-Ban Meta** | Camera + cloud AI assistant (reactive) | On-device reasoning + memory (proactive) | "Meta glasses ask Meta's cloud. Dan Glasses think for themselves." |

**Our defensible position:** We are the only team building a **proactive** wearable with an **open-source agent stack** and a **research pedigree** (papers, novel architectures) from **outside the US-China axis.**
